About the networkDivisorial and birational methods, uncovering hidden structure

The Divisorial Geometry Network is a Brazilian research network bringing together researchers in singularity theory, birational geometry, commutative algebra, algebraic topology, geometric analysis, partial differential equations and statistical learning. The group investigates how divisorial and birational methods uncover hidden geometric, topological, algebraic and analytical structures, fostering interactions between pure mathematics and emerging applications in data analysis and machine learning.

FocusResearch areas

I.

Singularity Theory

Resolution of singularities, Biracional geometry, Stratified spaces, Singularities of real and complex map-germs, Metric and topological invariants of singular spaces.

II.

Algebraic Topology

Persistent topology, Knots, Braids and topological invariants.

III.

Geometric Analysis

PDEs on singular spaces, Ssymptotic invariants, Morrey spaces and analytic aspects of singularities.

IV.

Statistical Learning

Geometric aspects of singular Bayesian statistics and machine learning.
