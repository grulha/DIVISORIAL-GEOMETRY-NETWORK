About

The Divisorial Geometry Network is a Brazilian research network bringing together researchers in singularity theory, birational geometry, commutative algebra, topology, geometric analysis, partial differential equations and statistical learning. The group seeks to investigate how divisorial and birational methods uncover hidden geometric, topological, algebraic and analytical structures, fostering interactions between pure mathematics and emerging applications in data analysis and machine learning.

Research Areas

Singularity Theory

Resolution of singularities, LCT, RLCT, Newton polyhedra, metric and topological invariants.

Algebraic Topology

Persistent topology, stratified spaces, knots, braids and topological invariants of singular spaces.

Geometric Analysis

PDEs on singular spaces, asymptotic invariants, Morrey spaces and analytic aspects of singularities.

Statistical Learning

Singular learning theory, Watanabe's theory and applications of birational invariants in machine learning.

Network

A Brazilian research network involving researchers from several institutions and all regions of Brazil, promoting collaborations, seminars, graduate training and international partnerships.

A commitment to diversity and equity. The team reflects an active commitment to diversity and equity in Brazilian science, composed of 62.5% women and 37.5% men researchers, with 31.3% of members self-declaring as Black. The group is coherent with the formation of a plural and representative scientific community, and its geographic distribution — spanning the Norte, Nordeste, Centro-Oeste, Sudeste and Sul regions — supports the goal of strengthening mathematical research capacity across all regions of Brazil.
